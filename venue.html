Arriving by train

To minimise the School‚Äôs environmental impact we strongly recommend that you travel to Venice by train whenever feasible. Venice‚Äôs main station‚ÄîVenezia Santa Lucia‚Äîis part of Italy‚Äôs excellent high speed train network and offers direct trains to many European cities (including Geneva, Munich, Vienna, and Zurich). A direct train brings you in 2.5h to Milan from where many other destinations in France, Germany, Switzerland and beyond can be reached. There are two train companies servicing Venice: Italo and Trenitalia. For schedules and more details, see the Trenitalia and Italo websites.

Transport from Santa Lucia Railway Station

In front of the railway station you find the services for water transports and the tickets stand. Many hotels can also easily be reached on foot. Some sightseeing on the way: the Grand Canal opens up in front of you as you exit the Station. Before descending the first step of the stone stairs, above your head a Murano glass aquarium reminds you of the richness of marine life. In 2018, before Covid, while getting the train to go to Zurich at ETH, Alma noticed that hundreds of fishes swam above her head in the Venice Station. The kaleidoscope of sea creatures in the transparency of glass reawakened Alma‚Äôs love for the sea, the joy of swimming, the passion for fishing.
