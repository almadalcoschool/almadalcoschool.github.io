Venue and Logistics

Venue

The School will take place in the historical Portego Room of Palazzo Cavalli-Franchetti, one of the two landmark buildings of the Istituto Veneto di Scienze, Lettere ed Arti. Palazzo Franchetti is located near the Ponte dell’Accademia bridge on the opposite side of the Canal Grande from the Accademia Galleries and Accademia vaporetto (water bus) station. Directions to Accademia or Ponte dell'Accademia are scattered throughout the city. If you get lost, ask for Accademia, but then remember to cross the bridge for Palazzo Franchetti. The Room of the School is located on the ground floor with full accessibility.

For further information about accessibility contact Laura Padoan.

Transports

Arriving by train

To minimise the School’s environmental impact we strongly recommend that you travel to Venice by train whenever feasible. Venice’s main station—Venezia Santa Lucia—is part of Italy’s excellent high speed train network and offers direct trains to many European cities (including Geneva, Munich, Vienna, and Zurich). A direct train brings you in 2.5h to Milan from where many other destinations in France, Germany, Switzerland and beyond can be reached. There are two train companies servicing Venice: Italo and Trenitalia. For schedules and more details, see the Trenitalia and Italo websites.

Transport from Santa Lucia Railway Station

In front of the railway station you find the services for water transports and the tickets stand. Many hotels can also easily be reached on foot. Some sightseeing on the way: the Grand Canal opens up in front of you as you exit the Station. Before descending the first step of the stone stairs, above your head a Murano glass aquarium reminds you of the richness of marine life. In 2018, before Covid, while getting the train to go to Zurich at ETH, Alma noticed that hundreds of fishes swam above her head in the Venice Station. In Alma the kaleidoscope of sea creatures in the transparency of glass reawakened the love for the sea, the joy of swimming, the passion for fishing.

Arriving by plane

Transport from Marco Polo airport

Water (direct) and road Taxis (via Piazzale Roma)

You can take a private water taxi directly to your final destination (which is an expensive choice, from the airport to Palazzo Franchetti it costs about €100.00). Alternatively, you can take a road taxi to Piazzale Roma, and then follow directions from Piazzale Roma (40 Euros).

Alilaguna waterbus service (via city center)

This company offers a service that goes from the airport to Murano, the Lido, the Arsenale with a final stop at San Marco. Tickets cost €15.00 and can be purchased in the arrival hall of the airport, directly on board, or online (€15.00). The service runs from 6 am until midnight. For timetables and further information visit Alilaguna (www.alilaguna.it). Then follow the directions to Accademia or take the vaporettos n. 1 or n. 2, direction Accademia.

ATVO buses (via Piazzale Roma)

These blue shuttle buses run from the airport to Piazzale Roma, leaving from the entrance of the departure hall. Tickets cost €10 one way, €18 return. Automatic ticket machines can be found in the baggage hall, in the arrivals hall, and outside the airport. The service runs from 4.20 am to 1.20 am and takes about 20 minutes to reach Venice. For timetables and further information visit ATVO. Then follow the directions to Accademia or take the vaporettos n. 1 or n. 2, direction Accademia.

ACTV Public bus n°5 (via Piazzale Roma)

Bus line n°5 runs every half hour. The last stop is "Piazzale Roma". Tickets cost €10 (€18 return) and can be purchased at the ACTV stand in the arrival hall. The service takes about 25 minutes to reach Venice. For timetables and further information visit ACTV. Then follow the directions to Accademia or take the vaporettos n. 1 or n. 2, direction Accademia.

Transport from Treviso airport

ATVO buses (via Piazzale Roma). Automatic ticket machines can be found in the baggage hall, in the arrivals hall, or you can buy your ticket on board. The service runs from 7.45 am to 10.30 pm and takes about 50 minutes to reach Venice. For timetables and further information visit ATVO. €12 one way, €22 return. Then follow the directions to Accademia or take the vaporettos n. 1 or n. 2, direction Accademia.

Arriving by car

If you drive to Venice, you will have to pay for a garage or parking space during your stay. The last points in Venice where you can leave your car are "Piazzale Roma" and "Tronchetto". Parking at "Piazzale Roma" in the Municipal Garage costs €24.00-29.00 for 24 hours, in the private Garage San Marco (next door) it costs €30.00 for 24 hours. To reach the Tronchetto parking area, as you come into Venice, follow the signs and turn right before "Piazzale Roma". Parking here costs €21.00 for 24 hours. (Do not leave valuables in the car. There is a luggage deposit office, open daily 8 a.m. - 8 p.m., next to the Pullman Bar on the ground floor of the Municipal Garage at "Piazzale Roma"). You can reach Piazzale Roma from Tronchetto through the People Mover. Another solution is to park your car in front of the train station in Mestre. Parking in Mestre costs much less (around € 5.00 - € 9.00 per day), and there are frequent trains and buses between Mestre and Venice Piazzale Roma. Then follow the directions from Piazzale Roma or from the train station to Accademia.
